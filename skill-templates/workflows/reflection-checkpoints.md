# Reflection Checkpoints

## Purpose

Force metacognitive pauses at key moments to ensure you're thinking critically about your literature review, not just generating text.

---

## When Checkpoints Trigger

| Moment | Checkpoint | Purpose |
|--------|------------|---------|
| After concept validation | Pre-Synthesis | Confirm direction |
| After literature synthesis | Post-Synthesis | Reflect on findings |
| After first draft | Pre-Audit | Self-assessment |
| After audit | Pre-Submission | Final reflection |

---

## Checkpoint 2: Post-Synthesis

**Triggered after:** Stage 2 literature synthesis complete

```
ðŸ”„ REFLECTION CHECKPOINT: Post-Synthesis

You've reviewed [N] sources. Reflect:

1. EXPECTATIONS vs REALITY
   What did you expect? _________________________________
   What did you find? _________________________________
   
2. SURPRISES
   What surprised you? _________________________________
   How do you explain it? _________________________________

3. PATTERNS
   What patterns emerged? _________________________________
   Are these real or artifacts of your search? _________________________________

4. GAPS
   What's missing from the literature? _________________________________
   Is this a real gap or just outside your search? _________________________________

5. ALTERNATIVE INTERPRETATIONS
   How else could you organize this literature? _________________________________
   What would change if you used a different framework? _________________________________

6. CONTRIBUTION CHECK
   Does your planned contribution still make sense? _________________________________
   Need to adjust? (yes/no): ___

Ready to proceed to drafting? (yes/need to revisit synthesis)
```

## Checkpoint 3: Pre-Audit

**Triggered after:** First complete draft

```
ðŸ”„ REFLECTION CHECKPOINT: Pre-Audit (Self-Assessment)

Before independent audit, assess your own work:

1. ARGUMENT STRENGTH
   On a scale of 1-10, how strong is your argument? ___
   What's the weakest part? _________________________________

2. EVIDENCE
   Which claims have weakest support? _________________________________
   What evidence would strengthen them? _________________________________

3. COUNTERARGUMENTS
   What would a skeptical reviewer say? _________________________________
   Have you addressed this? (yes/no): ___

4. VOICE CHECK
   Does this sound like you or like AI? _________________________________
   Which sections feel most/least authentic? _________________________________

5. CONTRIBUTION
   In one sentence, what's your contribution? _________________________________
   Is this clear in the draft? (yes/no): ___

6. WEAKNESSES
   What would YOU criticize about this draft? _________________________________

Self-assessment score: ___/10

Proceed to independent audit? (yes/revise first)
```

## Checkpoint 4: Pre-Submission

**Triggered after:** Audit complete, revisions done

```
ðŸ”„ REFLECTION CHECKPOINT: Pre-Submission

Final reflection before declaring done:

1. COMPLETENESS
   Is everything you intended to say in there? (yes/no): ___
   What's missing? _________________________________

2. AUDIENCE
   Who will read this? _________________________________
   Will they understand your argument? (yes/no): ___
   What might confuse them? _________________________________

3. CONTRIBUTION
   What will readers learn that they didn't know? _________________________________
   Why should they care? _________________________________

4. LIMITATIONS
   What can't you claim based on this review? _________________________________
   Is this acknowledged in the text? (yes/no): ___

5. INTEGRITY
   Are all citations accurate? (yes/verified/some uncertain): ___
   Any remaining [VERIFY] tags? ___

6. VOICE
   Does this represent YOUR thinking? (yes/no): ___
   Would you defend every claim? (yes/no): ___

7. READINESS
   On a scale of 1-10, how ready is this? ___
   What would make it a 10? _________________________________

Final decision:
[ ] Submit as is
[ ] Minor revisions needed
[ ] Major revisions needed
[ ] Not ready
```

## Reflection Prompts by Topic

### For Theory Selection
- Why this theory and not alternatives?
- What does this theory help you see?
- What does this theory obscure?

### For Literature Organization
- Why this structure and not others?
- What falls between categories?
- What's privileged by your organization?

### For Argument Construction
- What's the strongest counterargument?
- What evidence would disprove your claim?
- Who would disagree and why?

### For Voice/Style
- Does this sound like you or like AI?
- Which sentences feel most authentic?
- What would your advisor recognize as your voice?

For experienced researchers in Notes tier:

```

Skipping may result in more revision later.

Checkpoint skipped. Proceeding to next stage."
```

For Publication tier: Checkpoints cannot be skipped.
